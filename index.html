Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

The Founding Father and Former Prime Minister of Bangladesh

Here's a time line of the great person's life:

  • 1920 - Born in Tungipara, a village in Gopalganj District in the province of Bengal in British India
  • 1934 - Withdrew from school to undergo eye surgery.
  • 1938 - Returned to school owing to the severity of the surgery and slow recovery.
  • 1938 - Started contributing to politics.
  • 1939 - Joined a meeting with Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy that requested for the establishment of All India Muslim Students League.
  • 1939 - Was made the general secretary of The Muslim League when it was formed. He was also the secretary of the Muslim League's defense committee.
  • 1940 - Became politically active when he joined the All India Muslim Students Federation
  • 1941 - Elected as a councilor of Bengal and Faridpur district of the Bengal Muslim Students Federation.
  • 1941 - Passed his Matriculation from Gopalganj Missionary School
  • 1943 - Joined the Bengal Muslim League and worked actively for the League's cause of a separate Muslim state of Pakistan
  • 1944 - Intermediate of Arts from Islamia College (now Maulana Azad College)
  • 1946 - Went on to become general secretary of the Islamia College Students Union.
  • 1946 - working under Suhrawardy during the communal violence that broke out in Kolkata just before the partition of India, and joined the Bengal state movement with Suhrawardy.
  • 1947 - Bachelor of Arts from Islamia College (now Maulana Azad College)
  • 1948 - Enrolled in the University of Dhaka to study law and founded the East Pakistan Muslim Students' League.
  • 1948 - immediately decided to start a movement against this former planned decision of the Muslim League of adopting Urdu as the state language of Pakistan (21st March, 1948).
  • 1948 - Arrested in front of the secretariat building in 11 March, but due to pressure from the student protest, he was released on 15 March.
  • 1949 - Expelled from the university on the charge of 'inciting the fourth-class employees' in their agitation against the university authority's indifference towards their legitimate demands.
  • 1949 - Left the Muslim League to join Maulana Bhashani and Yar Mohammad Khan in the formation of the Awami Muslim League, the predecessor of the Awami League and elected joint secretary of its East Bengal unit in 1949.
  • 1953 - Was made the general secretary of the political party named Awami Muslim League
  • 1954 - Elected to the East Bengal Legislative Assembly on a United Front coalition ticket
  • 1954 - Serving briefly as the minister for agriculture during A. K. Fazlul Huq's government, Mujib was briefly arrested for organizing a protest of the central government's decision to dismiss the United Front ministry.
  • 1955 - Was elected to the second Constituent Assembly of Pakistan and served from 1955 to 1958.
  • 1956 - Entered a second coalition government as minister of industries, commerce, labour, anti-corruption and village aid
  • 1957 - Resigned from his ministry to work full-time for the party organisation.
  • 1958 - General Ayub Khan suspended the constitution and imposed martial law. Mujib was arrested for organizing resistance and imprisoned till 1961.
  • 1961 - Started organising an underground political body called the Swadhin Bangal Biplobi Parishad (Free Bangla Revolutionary Council), comprising student leaders, to oppose the regime of Ayub Khan.
  • 1962 - Was briefly arrested again for organizing protests
  • 1966 - Proclaimed a 6-point plan titled Our Charter of Survival at a national conference of opposition political parties at Lahore, in which he demanded self-government and considerable political, economic and defence autonomy for East Pakistan in a Pakistani federation with a weak central government.
  • 1971 - Called for independence and asked the people to launch a major campaign of civil disobedience and organized armed resistance at a mass gathering of people held at the Ramna Race Course Ground in Dhaka on 7th March, 1971.
  • 1971 - The Army launched Operation Searchlight to curb the political and civil unrest, fighting the nationalist militias that were believed to have received training in India. Speaking on radio even as the army began its crackdown, Mujib asked his fellows to create resistance against the Army occupation by telegraph at the midnight of 26 March 1971
  • 1971 - Was arrested at the midnight of 26th March, 1971 without charges and taken to West Pakistan after midnight from Tejgaon Airport on a PAF C-130 flight right under the noses of ATC Officer Squadron Leader Khaja, Senior Operations Officer Wing Commander Khademul Bashar and Director of Airport and Flight Security Squadron Leader M. Hamidullah Khan.
  • 1971 - Was elected as the first president of Bangladesh in 17th April, 1971 at Mujibnagar of the Meherpur district of former East Pakistan
  • 1972 - Was released at 10th January, 1972 from the jail after the independence of Bangladesh
  • 1972 - Took office as the prime minister of independent Bangladesh.
  • 1975 - On 15 August 1975, a group of junior army officers invaded the presidential residence with tanks and killed Mujib, his family (excluding Sheikh Hasina, the honorable prime minister of the Peoples' Republic of Bangladesh, 2009-present and Sheikh Rehena) and personal staff.
  • 2017 - On 30 October 2017, UNESCO recognised Mujib's 7 March Speech as part of the world's documentary heritage.
